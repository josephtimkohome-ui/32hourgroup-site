What You're Choosing

What Initiation Means

As an Initiate, you are in formation. Not performance. Formation. The goal is not to demonstrate mastery of the Doctrine — it is to practice it honestly, observe what happens, and repair what is neglected.

Initiation is not a commitment to perfection. It is a commitment to practice.

◆ Practice the Doctrine internally — not publicly, not performatively
◆ Observe how cycles work in your real, actual life
◆ Ask questions without pressure to get it right
◆ Accept gentle correction when distortion appears
✕ You are NOT expected to teach, recruit, or defend the Doctrine
✕ You are NOT expected to change your job, relationships, or lifestyle
Before You Enter

The Entry Vows

Read these before you join. You are not required to memorize them. They are here to set your orientation — and to remind you what you are choosing.

—I affirm that time, when lived with intention, is sufficient.
—I will not mistake urgency for importance.
—I commit to depth where depth is required.
—I will protect rest as essential, not optional.
—I accept responsibility for my attention.
—I will not impose this doctrine on others.
—I accept correction when distortion appears.
—These are for reflection. Not memorization.

Your Rights as an Initiate
  • Leave at any time, without explanation
  • Say no to questions or practices
  • Rest without justification
  • Ask for clarification at any time
  • Raise concerns about authority
  • Practice at your own pace
Retaliation or pressure is not permitted — ever.

Live accordingly.
